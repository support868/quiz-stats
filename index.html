Actionable insights
Key patterns and recommendations from the data
Audience Profile
You're talking to older women who've tried everything
84% female, 91% aged 55+, 90% have smoked 20+ years. This is a highly experienced, deeply habituated audience — not beginners. Generic messaging won't land.
55+
Top Motivator
Money + breath are tied as the #1 goal
72% selected both "save money" and "breathe easier." Lead with financial savings (avg £236/mo = £2,800/yr) and the immediate physical relief of breathing. Family (69%) and freedom (69%) are close behind.
£2,832
Failed Methods
Most have already tried patches, willpower & Chantix
67% tried patches, 62% tried willpower, 51% tried Chantix. Positioning Nordic Goods as different from these proven failures is critical. Don't compete — differentiate.
3+
Trigger Windows
Meals, morning coffee and stress = the triple threat
83% are triggered after meals, 76% by stress, 75% by coffee. These are precise ritual moments. Product messaging and habit-replacement strategies should target these exact windows.
3× daily
Core Fear
Anxiety is the #1 barrier — ahead of withdrawal
27% cite anxiety as their hardest challenge, beating physical withdrawal (24%) and habit (24%). Address anxiety and emotional support directly in copy and product positioning.
#1
Identity Pain
62% feel out of control — shame is a live signal
The top life impact is feeling out of control (62%), followed by breathlessness (60%) and cough (51%). 24% are hiding their smoking. Copy should acknowledge the shame without amplifying it.
62%
Purchase Intent
Motivation is high — 69% rate it 8–10/10
45% gave themselves a perfect 10. This is a warm, ready-to-act audience. Reduce friction in the funnel — they want to quit, they just need the right solution and reassurance.
45%
Repeat Tryers
32% have lost count of attempts — don't make them feel like failures
Another 45% have tried 3–10 times. This audience is persistent but battle-scarred. Messaging should validate their previous attempts and reframe this product as a fresh, science-backed approach — not "just another try."
77%
